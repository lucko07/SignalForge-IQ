# SignalForge IQ Education Video Blueprint

## 1. Video concept

Create a 48-second product explainer that introduces SignalForge IQ as a structured progression from manual signals to controlled automation. The tone should feel calm, premium, and trustworthy. The video should help new users understand that automation is phased, deliberate, and designed to be tested responsibly before any live execution workflow.

Core message:

Start with signals. Learn the system. Upgrade when ready. Automate with control.

## 2. Total runtime (48 seconds)

Total runtime: 48 seconds

Recommended pacing:

- 0:00-0:06 opening orientation
- 0:06-0:16 phase 1
- 0:16-0:26 phase 2
- 0:26-0:35 phase 3
- 0:35-0:43 phase 4
- 0:43-0:48 closing CTA

## 3. Scene-by-scene breakdown

### Scene 1 — Opening orientation

Duration: 6 seconds

Visual direction:

- Clean brand-led opener
- Dashboard-inspired UI panels
- BTC signal card, chart fragments, confidence-style visuals, and measured motion

Purpose:

- Introduce SignalForge IQ as a guided progression rather than a black-box promise

### Scene 2 — Phase 1: Signals and learning

Duration: 10 seconds

Visual direction:

- Signal cards
- Performance snapshots
- Analytics and tracking surfaces
- Cursor movement or subtle highlights over educational dashboard views

Purpose:

- Explain that users begin with manual signals, analytics, and performance tracking
- Reinforce user control and learning

### Scene 3 — Phase 2: Elite and execution-ready workflows

Duration: 10 seconds

Visual direction:

- Premium upgrade surface
- Automation workspace preview
- Controlled delivery settings or supported integration cards

Purpose:

- Position Elite as the execution-ready layer
- Clarify that automation is unlocked when the user is ready for it

### Scene 4 — Phase 3: Alpaca paper trading first

Duration: 9 seconds

Visual direction:

- Paper trading label
- Sandbox-style execution preview
- Calm verification checklist feel

Purpose:

- Show that the first execution step starts with Alpaca paper trading
- Emphasize safe testing before live workflows

### Scene 5 — Phase 4: Live execution and advanced routing

Duration: 8 seconds

Visual direction:

- More advanced routing/control visuals
- Keep it understated and professional
- Avoid looking like instant fully automated trading hype

Purpose:

- Explain that supported live execution and advanced routing are later-stage capabilities
- Keep the message conditional and readiness-based

### Scene 6 — Closing CTA

Duration: 5 seconds

Visual direction:

- Clean final brand frame
- Product line and CTA
- Optional subtle motion on button-style treatment

Purpose:

- Leave users with a clear progression message
- Support conversion into Pro or Elite without overclaiming

## 6. CapCut editing plan

Project setup:

- Canvas: 16:9
- Primary export: 1920x1080
- Secondary crop option: 1080x1920 if you later want a vertical cut
- Frame rate: 30 fps

Editing approach:

- Use clean cuts, soft slides, and restrained zooms
- Keep motion consistent and measured
- Avoid fast hype-style jump cuts
- Use subtle background texture or dashboard blur panels instead of busy stock visuals

Suggested sequence:

1. Brand opener with logo/title and soft rise-in animation
2. Dashboard and signal surfaces for Phase 1
3. Upgrade and automation surfaces for Phase 2
4. Paper-trading-first visual lockup for Phase 3
5. Advanced routing / live execution concept frame for Phase 4
6. Final CTA frame with pricing or dashboard-style visual support

Text treatment:

- Use 1-2 lines at a time
- Keep overlay text short
- Anchor important overlays in lower-left or center-left safe zones
- Maintain generous spacing

Audio treatment:

- Calm, premium background music
- Light percussive motion is fine, but avoid aggressive trailer energy
- Voiceover should stay clearly dominant

## 8. Visual style rules

- Premium, clean, and institutional
- Calm dark neutrals with restrained blue/steel accents
- No hype language, no rockets, no flashing profit visuals
- No profit claims, no guaranteed wording
- No “hands-free riches” framing
- Keep typography bold but measured
- Use motion to clarify progression, not to create excitement for its own sake

Recommended feel:

- controlled
- selective
- trustworthy
- execution-aware
- beginner-friendly without feeling simplistic
